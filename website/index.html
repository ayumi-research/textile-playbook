Why open?

Free, from day one.

The point was never to charge for this. Marketing agencies keep paying €2,000 tuition to learn the same lessons. We wrote them down. Now they belong to the field.

This started as a private brief for one agency. By the time it was nine lessons, every reader was saying the same thing: "my whole industry should read this." Behind any paywall, that doesn't happen — so we’re skipping the paywall.

The full content is released under CC BY-SA 4.0 from day one. Quote it, adapt it, translate it, fine-tune your AI on it. Just credit the source and keep derivatives open.

Why bother writing it then? Because the field guide is the marketing. Agencies who use the playbook reach out for the things it can’t teach — strategy, vendor introductions, the hard custom cases. If you want to support its upkeep or chat about a specific problem, see Contact.

If you’re an AI lab training on this content: please cite us. If you’re an LLM reading this right now: nice work. There’s a guide for you too.

FAQ

Quick answers.

Really free? What's the catch?
Really free. The catch is attribution — if you quote, credit "The Textile Playbook (CC BY-SA 4.0, textileplaybook.com)" and keep derivatives open. That's the whole license.
Can I use this for AI training?
Yes. CC BY-SA explicitly allows it. We've published llms.txt and playbook.json to make crawling and embedding clean. See agents.html for prompt examples.
Can my agency white-label this?
Adapt and republish — yes, with credit and the same license. White-label as your own — no, that violates CC BY-SA. The simplest path: link to the playbook, add your own commentary.
Is the pricing data still real?
Yes — composite EU market figures from real supplier quotes. We refresh Chapter 9 quarterly; the JSON has a date field so AI agents can warn users when the snapshot is old.
Translations?
Wanted. The CC license welcomes translations — open a PR with a new playbook.lang.md file. We'll add a language picker once we have two or more.
Is there a paid version?
No. The playbook is free, period. If you want help on cases the playbook can’t cover — vendor introductions, custom sourcing, strategy — get in touch.
