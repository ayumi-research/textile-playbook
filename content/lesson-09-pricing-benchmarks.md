## The Truth About Stitch Counts

Most buyers don't know their logo's stitch count. Most suppliers know this — and price accordingly. Industry-standard pricing is **per 1,000 stitches**, but this number is often hidden behind flat rates or tiered brackets.

**What matters more than exact stitch counts:**
- Logo size (chest vs. back vs. cap)
- Complexity (simple text vs. detailed artwork)
- Thread colors (more colors = more machine time)
- Garment type (thick jackets take longer than thin polos)

**Rule of thumb for stitch estimates:**
- Small chest text/logo: 1,500–3,000 stitches
- Medium chest logo: 3,000–7,000 stitches
- Large back logo: 10,000–20,000+ stitches
- Cap logo: 2,000–8,000 stitches (depends on size/curvature)

## How Pricing Actually Works

### Three Pricing Models You'll Encounter

**1. Per-Stitch Pricing**
- Transparent but complex
- Typical range: €0.50–€2.00 per 1,000 stitches
- Better for large orders where small differences matter
- Requires you to know (or trust) the stitch count

**2. Flat Tiered Pricing**
- Most common approach
- Fixed price per location (chest/back/cap) regardless of stitch count
- Volume discounts at breakpoints (20/50/100/200+ pieces)
- Simpler to quote, easier to compare

**3. Dynamic/Hybrid Pricing**
- Flat rate up to a threshold (e.g., 10 pieces), then dynamic
- Per-order fixed costs + per-piece variable costs
- Better reflects true economics: setup time, quality control, handling

### What's Bundled vs. What's Extra

**Should be included (red flags if itemized separately):**
- Digitizing/programming the embroidery file
- Thread color matching
- Basic quality check (all pieces reviewed)
- Backing removal and thread trimming
- Packaging for shipment

**Often extra (legitimate):**
- Rush turnaround (under 5 business days)
- Special thread types (metallic, glow-in-dark)
- Puff/3D foam embroidery
- Individual personalization (names/numbers)
- Shipping (standard vs. express)

**Premium services worth paying for:**
- Pre-production sampling (see it before they run it)
- Prototype approval process
- Per-piece photo documentation
- Dedicated account management
- Post-production QC with defect reports

## Rule-of-Thumb Pricing Benchmarks

These are **general EU market ranges**, not gospel. Use them for sanity checks:

### Small Logo (Chest/Beanie)
- **10 pieces:** €6–15 per piece
- **20 pieces:** €4–9 per piece
- **50 pieces:** €3–7 per piece
- **100+ pieces:** €2.50–5 per piece

### Cap Logo
- **10 pieces:** €8–18 per piece
- **20 pieces:** €5–12 per piece
- **50 pieces:** €4–9 per piece
- **100+ pieces:** €3–7 per piece

### Large Back Logo
- **10 pieces:** €15–50 per piece (highly variable by complexity)
- **20 pieces:** €8–25 per piece
- **50 pieces:** €6–18 per piece
- **100+ pieces:** €5–15 per piece

**Below these ranges:** Proceed with caution. Might indicate cutting corners (no QC, no cleanup, low-quality backing) or hidden fees.

**Above these ranges:** Could be premium service, high-cost market (Switzerland/Nordics), or a supplier who doesn't want your business.

## Finding Your Sweet Spot: Supplier Archetypes

Not every embroiderer is right for every job. Understanding the four supplier types saves you time and mismatched expectations.

### Type A: The Solo Operator
**Profile:** 1–2 single-head machines, often home-based or small workshop
**Sweet spot:** 1–20 pieces
**Strengths:** Personal attention, flexible on custom requests, quick turnarounds on small runs
**Weaknesses:** Limited capacity, inconsistent availability, may lack professional processes
**Price range:** Lowest for micro-orders, loses competitiveness above 50 pieces
**Best for:** Samples, prototypes, last-minute gifts, highly customized one-offs

### Type B: The Boutique Shop
**Profile:** 4–12 multi-head machines, small team (3–10 people)
**Sweet spot:** 20–500 pieces
**Strengths:** Professional service, quality control, prototyping capability, account management
**Weaknesses:** Higher overhead reflected in pricing, may decline very small orders
**Price range:** Mid-market, premium for service level
**Best for:** Regular business orders, brand-conscious clients, complex designs requiring back-and-forth

### Type C: The Volume House
**Profile:** 20+ machines, dedicated production floor, streamlined processes
**Sweet spot:** 200–5,000+ pieces
**Strengths:** Aggressive pricing at scale, consistent quality, reliable timelines
**Weaknesses:** High minimums, rigid processes, limited customization, slow response times
**Price range:** Lowest per-unit at volume, may refuse small orders entirely
**Best for:** Uniform rollouts, event merchandise, established designs with no changes

### Type D: The Eastern EU Outsourcer
**Profile:** Poland, Romania, Bulgaria-based production
**Sweet spot:** 500–10,000+ pieces
**Strengths:** 40–70% cost savings at scale, EU quality standards
**Weaknesses:** Shipping complexity, longer lead times, communication barriers, import formalities
**Price range:** Significantly lower, but add shipping + import costs
**Best for:** Price-sensitive bulk orders where 2–3 week lead time is acceptable

## How to Choose Your Supplier Type

**Ask yourself:**

1. **What's my typical order size?**
   - Mostly 1–10 pieces → Solo operator or boutique with low minimums
   - Regularly 20–200 pieces → Boutique (Type B)
   - 500+ pieces routine → Volume house or Eastern EU

2. **How much hand-holding do I need?**
   - Want samples, photos, approvals → Boutique or higher-end solo
   - Know exactly what you want, just execute → Volume house

3. **What's my quality tolerance?**
   - "Every piece must be perfect" → Pay for Type B's QC
   - "Occasional defect is acceptable at this price" → Volume house or Eastern EU

4. **How complex is my design?**
   - Simple text/logos → Anyone can handle
   - Gradients, fine detail, special effects → Boutique or specialist only

5. **What's my timeline?**
   - Under 1 week → Boutique or solo (if they have capacity)
   - 2–4 weeks → Volume house
   - 4–8 weeks → Eastern EU feasible

## Red Flags: When to Walk Away

**Pricing red flags:**
- Refuses to give a price range without seeing the design (everyone can estimate)
- Significantly below market (under €0.50/1k stitches or €3 for small logos) — corners are being cut
- Significantly above market without explaining the premium
- Itemizes "thread cost" or "machine time" separately (should be bundled)

**Service red flags:**
- No mention of quality check or approval process
- Won't provide a sample before production run
- "We don't do backing removal" (you'll spend hours cleaning thread)
- Vague on turnaround time
- Hard to reach before you place the order (gets worse after)

**Communication red flags:**
- Takes >48 hours to respond to initial inquiry
- Vague answers to specific questions
- Won't provide references or portfolio
- "Just send us the files and we'll handle it" (without discussing details)

## The Real Cost Calculation

**Price per piece × Quantity = Sticker price**

**Sticker price + Your time + Risk of rework + Stress = True cost**

A quote that's 20% higher but includes:
- Pre-production sample
- Per-piece QC
- Photo confirmation
- Clean finishing (no backing removal on your end)
- Replacement of defects

...is often cheaper than the rock-bottom quote that delivers 15% defects and requires 10 hours of your time to fix.

## Action Checklist

**Before getting quotes:**
- [ ] Know your approximate stitch count (or at least logo size)
- [ ] Have your design file ready (vector preferred: .ai, .eps, .svg)
- [ ] Know your timeline (rush vs. standard)
- [ ] Decide your tolerance for defects (0%, <5%, <10%)

**In the quote request:**
- [ ] Specify quantity clearly
- [ ] Attach design file
- [ ] State timeline needs
- [ ] Ask what's included (setup, QC, backing removal, shipping)
- [ ] Ask about minimum order and price breaks

**When evaluating quotes:**
- [ ] Compare total project cost, not just per-piece
- [ ] Check what services are bundled vs. extra
- [ ] Verify turnaround and approval process
- [ ] Ask about defect policy
- [ ] Request references or portfolio examples

## Key Takeaways

1. **Price per stitch is a fiction.** Price per *outcome* is what matters.

2. **Match your order size to the supplier's sweet spot.** Solo operators for tiny runs, boutiques for mid-size, volume houses for bulk.

3. **Bundled services save money.** Itemized cheap quotes often cost more in time and defects.

4. **Benchmarks are sanity checks, not targets.** If you're far below, ask why. If you're far above, ask what's included.

5. **The cheapest quote is rarely the best value.** Professional communication, QC, and finishing are worth paying for.
